Nutrish

A comprehensive solution designed to support individuals dealing with anorexia by helping them understand their condition, monitor their journey towards recovery, and keep a record of their daily routines

Overview

Several young individuals experience symptoms or have been diagnosed with Anorexia Nervosa, a serious eating disorder. As part of a UI/UX team we conducted user research to understand the needs of these individuals, and their willingness to use an app for help in dealing with their condition. Our findings revealed a desire for an integrated self-diagnosis and post-diagnosis app. Awareness of the disorder was also often lacking until it became severe, contributing to relapses post-treatment. Based on this research, we created a lo-fi prototype, tested it, and progressed to a hi-fi version. Our user testing indicated strong appreciation for the final app, with minor design adjustments.

Role: Team Lead

Tools & methods: Google Forms, Adobe Creative Suite, Research Interviews, Figma, Usability Testing

Team: HCI Course Project at LUMS (5 team members)

Timeline: 4 months (Sept 2020 - Dec 2020)

Toolkit

The Problem

“How to tackle raising awareness of mental health issues among high school students in Pakistan to encourage seeking therapeutic treatment?

Despite the prevalence of mental illnesses in high school students, most of those who require mental health treatment do not receive it. Although the therapeutic bond is an important factor in therapeutic efficacy, it is underrepresented in digital treatments. Existing research suggests that a therapeutic relationship is associated with higher engagement and adherence. Because of privacy concerns, high treatment costs, time constraints, a misguided emphasis on mental health, and a scarcity of resources, many high school students do not seek help for mental health issues.

The Process

User Research

Starting with some literature review, we quickly found out this was an underreached topic in Pakistan. To tackle this, we conducted surveys in public medical forums and gathered our initial data along with a group of participants who were diagnosed patients that helped us throughout our project to build the application. We built user personas and user stories from the initial interviews we conducted and the survey data we gathered. Some paper sketches were designed based on these, with multiple ideas about tackling the awareness situation. The main design alternatives included different interfaces using websites, various application layouts, and multiple features. After iterations of this process for different problems, a mobile application was decided upon as the final design. Once our design was complete, we tested the application and evaluated its effectiveness

Storyboards

Through storyboarding, we visualized specific scenarios and interactions that potential users might encounter while engaging with our gamified product. These captivating narratives enabled us to envisage the entire user journey, highlighting pivotal touchpoints and potential pain points along the way. By translating our insights into compelling user stories, we deepened our understanding of the user experience and identified key opportunities for improvement.

User Personas

Design Prototype

We designed a gamified mobile app centered around a personified avatar. The gamification element included achievements, unlockable, and level ups. The idea was to pitch it as a fitness app, so we strictly excluded any mention of anorexia.

Testing

Lastly, we tested off the application by sharing the prototype with our some old participants and a few new ones. This testing was supported by interviews/surveys, where the results showed that the application was mostly liked by the users, with some of them requesting new features, and some visual changes to make the application more appealing.

Key Takeaways

- Overall feedback was positive, with users willing to readily adopt and incorporate the application into their daily lives. Out of the multiple core learnings, a surprising one was that a majority of our user base was aware that they had an issue but were unable to tackle it due to a lack of accessible guidance in Pakistan.
- Following this, an exploration of the main issues to adoption faced by an application explicitly tackling eating disorders showed that calorie tracking applications lead to an unhealthy obsession and are often impersonalized. They are, therefore, ineffective.
- Next, we concluded that our users required a solution that helped them medically diagnose an eating disorder and put them on their journey to recovery. Personalized coaching and access to therapy were two key features that were highly regarded.
- One way to ensure that we optimally maintained our users’ engagement level was to focus on the interface and design, which would ensure that the application was “personalized”.

Self Reflection

As my first major design project, there were various key learnings. I became familiar with designing user-centered applications, including scenario building and analysis and conducting user research. The significance of low-fidelity prototypes to test ideas also became apparent. Other things I learned included the importance of backing user flows with primary research which is only possible through genuine interactions and strict confidentiality agreements.
